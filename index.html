Lamborghini V12 Flagship Lineage

The ultimate expression of Italian performance and design.

Miura

The Miura, introduced in 1966, is widely regarded as the first true supercar. With its transversely mounted V12 and stunning Bertone design, it redefined what was possible on the road.

  • Engine: 3.9L V12
  • Power: 350 hp
  • Top Speed: 280 km/h
  • Years Produced: 1966–1973

Countach

With radical wedge-shaped styling and scissor doors, the Countach became a poster icon. It pushed boundaries with raw power and futuristic aesthetics.

  • Engine: 5.2L V12
  • Power: 455 hp (LP5000 QV)
  • Top Speed: 295 km/h
  • Years Produced: 1974–1990
    • Click for More Info

Diablo

The Diablo was Lamborghini’s first 200+ mph car, ushering the brand into the modern age of supercars with aggressive styling and monumental performance.

  • Engine: 5.7L–6.0L V12
  • Power: Up to 575 hp (GT)
  • Top Speed: 325 km/h
  • Years Produced: 1990–2001
    • Click for More Info

Murciélago

The Murciélago carried the V12 torch into the 21st century with a dramatic redesign and fierce performance. It was the first V12 Lamborghini under Audi’s ownership.

  • Engine: 6.2L–6.5L V12
  • Power: Up to 670 hp (LP670-4 SV)
  • Top Speed: 342 km/h
  • Years Produced: 2001–2010
    • Click for More Info

Aventador SVJ

The Aventador SVJ is the ultimate evolution of Lamborghini’s pure V12 lineage. Track-focused, aerodynamically advanced, and explosively powerful.

  • Engine: 6.5L V12
  • Power: 770 hp
  • Top Speed: 350 km/h
  • Years Produced: 2019–2022
    • Click for More Info

Revuelto

The Revuelto marks the dawn of a new era—Lamborghini’s first V12 hybrid hypercar. It merges electrified technology with the soul of a raging bull.

  • Engine: 6.5L V12 Hybrid
  • Power: 1,015 hp (combined)
  • Top Speed: >350 km/h
  • Production: 2023–
